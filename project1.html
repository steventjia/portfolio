Web Design for Real Estate Company, Singapore

About The Project

This is a freelance project that I received from a Singaporean client. Buycondo is a Singaporean real estate company that focuses on selling apartments. Tasked with this project, my primary objective is to redesign the current website, infusing it with a modern touch, and strategically expanding its reach to a larger user and buyer base. In my role as the solo UI/UX designer for this endeavor, my client has graciously granted me the creative freedom to share and implement my innovative ideas, aiming to elevate the current design to new heights."

Objectives

  1. Revamp the current website design to achieve a more modern look and feel.
  2. Increase the website's appeal and usability to attract a larger audience of users and potential buyers.
  3. Optimize the website for improved search engine visibility and cross-platform accessibility.
  4. Encourage innovative ideas and design enhancements from the solo UI/UX designer to elevate the user experience.

Methodology

For The UX Research Methods, i use both Quantitative Researchto gather informations from studies, reviews from users who used other competitors’ real estate websites and also Qualitative Research to do competitor analysis to find the main problems and to compare strength and weakness from each other’s real estate websites (Propertyguru, Verde Two, iProperty.com).

UX Research Process

I keep it simple and efficient in enhancing the user experience. First, I analyze the website to spot usability issues in navigation, information structure, and user flow. Then, I analyze the reviews from similar website users to uncover problems and gather feedback. This research extends to finding a new logo name and its meaning. Using this valuable insight, I redesign the website, focusing on making it more user-friendly and visually appealing. The goal is to ensure a better experience for property buyers and sellers.

What key features should the website offer to make it easy and efficient for users in Singapore?

The website should organize property listings in a clear and user-friendly manner. Properties should be categorized by type (e.g., condos, landed houses), and each listing should include essential details such as price, location, number of bedrooms, and square footage. Additionally, a straightforward navigation menu or sections dedicated to different property types can help users quickly find what they're looking for.

Additionally, for future improvements, the website should incorporate features such as a robust search filter system that allows users to refine their searches based on criteria like location, price range, property type, and amenities.

How can the website best showcase property listings to attract potential buyers and sellers in the Singapore real estate market?

To effectively showcase property listings and resonate with millennials and Gen Z users in the Singapore real estate market, the website should adopt a sleek and contemporary design. This includes employing a clean and minimalist user interface with high-quality images and videos that highlight property details. Implementing responsive design for seamless mobile browsing is crucial, as younger users often access websites on their smartphones. Additionally, incorporating social media integration and interactive features, such as virtual tours or 360-degree views, can enhance user engagement and appeal to the tech-savvy preferences of these demographics.

The Process

1. Website References

For website references, I studied and compared the landing pages of real estate companies, particularly those in Singapore. Specifically, I analyzed PropertyGuru.com.sg, 99.co, Verde Indonesia, and iProperty.com.sg. I assessed their design, user-friendliness, load times, placement of contents, visuals, call-to-action buttons, and any standout features.

2. Challenges & Possible Solutions

The primary challenges in this website redesign involve enhancing the current designs, improving typography, and elevating the overall mood of the site. This encompasses creating a modern, user-friendly layout, optimizing font choices for improved readability, and utilizing visuals to evoke specific emotions.

In addition to these challenges, there is a need to create a straightforward landing page without any search bar or filter function. There's also the task of developing a fresh brand name and logo design. While I believe that the search bar and filter function are crucial features, the client opted to omit them from this design due to the additional development required.

As for potential solutions, I have crafted a new design characterized by a minimalist and contemporary aesthetic. In the process, I brainstormed new names and ultimately selected "Skyline.sg" as the new brand name.
