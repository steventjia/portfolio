Web Design for GOTO Impact Foundation, Indonesia

About The Project

This is a project that I received from FAB Indonesia, a creative agency based in Jakarta, Indonesia. At FAB Indonesia, I had the opportunity to design for Indonesian big brands, and GOTO Impact Foundation is one of them. GOTO Impact is a product of GOTO Indonesia, a startup company that focuses on transportation and e-commerce.

GOTO Impact Foundation is a purpose-driven impact catalyst that builds impact innovation ecosystems to advance collective progress toward a sustainable civilization. By cultivating co-evolving networks that foster continuous innovation, we connect sector experts and on-ground communities, synchronize all impact players through data and capacity building, and scale up innovative solutions for greater impact.

Objectives

  1. Enhance User Experience: Ensure that the website's design is user-friendly, with intuitive navigation, clear calls to action, and an overall pleasant user experience.
  2. Compact Information Presentation: Organize and present information in a concise and easily digestible manner, ensuring that visitors can quickly find the key details they're looking for without overwhelming them with unnecessary content.
  3. Visual Consistency: Create a cohesive visual identity, including consistent branding elements such as colors, fonts, and imagery, to establish a strong and memorable visual presence.
  4. Accessibility: Prioritize accessibility by ensuring that the website is designed to accommodate users with disabilities, adhering to accessibility standards such as WCAG (Web Content Accessibility Guidelines).

Methodology

For The UX Research Methods, i use both Quantitative Research to gather informations from studies, reviews from users who used other competitors’ foundation landing page and also Qualitative Research to do competitor analysis to find the main problems and to compare strength and weakness from each other’s foundation landing page (YKIP, GOTO Company, Teach from Canada).

UX Research Process

I'm focused on creating a solid foundation for a compact landing page. I thoroughly examine the current GOTO company website, YKIP, and Teach for Canada competitors' websites. My goal is to spot and fix any issues related to how users navigate, find information, and move through the page. I also gather feedback through usability tests and interviews. I research new logo names and their meanings to inform our branding efforts. This way, we ensure a better experience for property buyers and sellers.

What are the primary goals and actions we want visitors to take on our website landing page?

Our primary goal for the landing page is to effectively communicate our mission and engage visitors in our cause. We want them to understand the social issues we address and the impact of our work. To achieve this, we aim to present clear and compelling content that showcases our mission, vision, and values. We'll prominently feature success stories, case studies, and statistics that demonstrate the positive change we bring to communities. Our landing page will also provide easy access to relevant information, such as our programs and initiatives, and clear contact details for inquiries. By creating an appealing and user-friendly interface, we intend to guide visitors towards a deeper understanding of our mission and inspire them to support our cause.

What information is most important for our visitors to access easily, and how can we prioritize and present it effectively?

Regarding the information that is most important for our visitors to access easily, we prioritize key elements such as our mission statement, the social issues we address, and the impact of our work. Our landing page will prominently display these core messages to ensure visitors grasp our organization's purpose and the significance of their support. Additionally, we'll feature success stories, testimonials, and visuals that highlight our achievements. The content will be organized in a logical and visually appealing manner to guide visitors' attention effectively. We aim to create an engaging and informative experience that empowers visitors to connect with our mission and contribute to our cause. Our navigation menu will also be user-friendly and readily accessible, enabling visitors to explore different aspects of our organization effortlessly.

The Process

1. Website References

For website references, I studied and compared the landing pages of foundation companies, particularly those in Indonesia. Specifically, I analyzed YKIP, GOTO Indonesia, Teach for Canada. I assessed their design, user-friendliness, load times, placement of contents, visuals, call-to-action buttons, and any standout features.

2. Challenges & Possible Solutions

Creating the GOTO Impact Foundation landing page involves some interesting challenges and opportunities. The key is to make sure our website helps both kids and small businesses, all while aiding those affected by natural disasters and pandemics. To tackle this, we need to make our message clear and engaging for kids, while maintaining a professional appeal to attract small businesses that can offer financial support. Another challenge is making the website easy to use for everyone, including kids and adults. We can use fun pictures and activities to engage kids, while providing clear information about what we do to attract small businesses. We'll also highlight how our efforts support those affected by natural disasters and pandemics to create a well-rounded impact. By addressing these challenges, we can create a GOTO Impact Foundation landing page that works for everyone and achieves our mission.
